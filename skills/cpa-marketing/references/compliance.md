# CPA Compliance Guide

## Legal Requirements

### FTC Disclosure (USA)

**Required by law for affiliate marketing in the US.**

#### Where to Disclose
- Before affiliate links
- In video descriptions
- On landing pages
- In social media posts
- In email marketing

#### Disclosure Examples

**For Social Media:**
```
#ad #sponsored
#affiliate
"This post contains affiliate links"
"I earn from qualifying purchases"
```

**For Websites:**
```
"Affiliate Disclosure: We may earn a commission if you click 
links on this page and make a purchase. This comes at no 
additional cost to you."
```

**For Videos:**
```
"This video is sponsored by [Brand]. Some links are affiliate 
links that help support the channel."
```

#### FTC Guidelines
- Clear and conspicuous
- Close to the claim
- Easy to understand
- Not buried in fine print
- Visible before user acts

### GDPR (European Union)

**Required if targeting EU users.**

#### Key Requirements
1. **Cookie Consent** - Must get explicit consent before setting cookies
2. **Privacy Policy** - Clear, accessible privacy policy
3. **Data Rights** - Users can request their data, deletion, etc.
4. **Data Protection** - Secure handling of personal data

#### Cookie Banner Example
```html
<div id="cookie-banner">
    <p>We use cookies to improve your experience and track conversions.
       By clicking "Accept", you consent to our use of cookies.</p>
    <button onclick="acceptCookies()">Accept</button>
    <button onclick="rejectCookies()">Reject</button>
    <a href="/privacy">Learn More</a>
</div>
```

#### Privacy Policy Must Include
- What data you collect
- Why you collect it
- How you use it
- Who you share it with
- How users can contact you
- Cookie usage details

---

### CCPA (California)

**Required if targeting California users.**

#### Key Requirements
1. "Do Not Sell My Info" link
2. Privacy policy with CA-specific disclosures
3. Right to opt-out of data sales
4. Right to access and delete data

## Platform Compliance

### Social Media Rules

#### Instagram
- Must disclose affiliate relationships
- No misleading claims
- Follow community guidelines
- No spam or bot activity
- Hashtag rules (#ad required)

#### TikTok
- Disclose partnerships
- No misleading content
- Authentic engagement only
- Follow advertising policies
- Age-appropriate content

#### YouTube
- Paid promotion disclosure (built-in)
- Affiliate links in description allowed
- No misleading thumbnails
- Follow ad policies
- Content rating required

#### Facebook
- Ad disclosure required for paid ads
- Affiliate links allowed in organic posts
- No misleading claims
- Follow advertising policies
- Authentic engagement only

#### Twitter/X
- Clear disclosure (#ad or "affiliate")
- No spam or bot behavior
- Authentic engagement
- Follow advertising policies

## Content Locking Compliance

### Do's ✅
- Provide real value behind the lock
- Clear instructions on how to unlock
- Accurate preview of content
- Reasonable offer requirements
- Working download/access

### Don'ts ❌
- Fake content (promising what you don't have)
- Misleading instructions
- Excessive offer requirements
- Non-working links after unlock
- Malware or harmful files
- Copyrighted content without permission

## Email Marketing Compliance

### CAN-SPAM Act (USA)

#### Requirements
1. **Accurate header info** - Valid "From" name and email
2. **Valid subject line** - Must reflect content
3. **Physical address** - Include in every email
4. **Clear unsubscribe** - Working opt-out link
5. **Honor opt-outs** - Within 10 business days
6. **Identify as ad** - Clear disclosure

#### Penalties
- Up to $50,120 per email violation
- Criminal penalties for severe violations

### Email Best Practices
- Double opt-in for lists
- Clear unsubscribe in every email
- Physical mailing address in footer
- Identify sender clearly
- No misleading subject lines

---

## Prohibited Practices

### Never Do These
1. ❌ **Click fraud** - Clicking your own links, using bots
2. ❌ **Lead fraud** - Fake signups, incentived without disclosure
3. ❌ **Content theft** - Copyrighted material without permission
4. ❌ **False advertising** - Misleading claims about offers
5. ❌ **Spam** - Unsolicited emails, messages, comments
6. ❌ **Brand impersonation** - Pretending to be official source
7. ❌ **Incentivized fraud** - Paying users to complete offers without disclosure
8. ❌ **Hidden terms** - Burying important information

### Consequences
- **Account bans** - From networks, platforms
- **Payment withheld** - Networks won't pay
- **Legal action** - FTC, lawsuits
- **Reputation damage** - Permanent blacklisting

## Network Terms Compliance

### Common Network Rules
1. **No incent** unless specifically allowed
2. **No trademark bidding** on brand keywords
3. **Traffic source restrictions** - Check what's allowed
4. **Geo restrictions** - Don't send wrong traffic
5. **Creative restrictions** - Use approved ads only
6. **Quality standards** - Maintain conversion quality

### Always Read
- Network terms of service
- Offer-specific restrictions
- Traffic source requirements
- Payment terms
